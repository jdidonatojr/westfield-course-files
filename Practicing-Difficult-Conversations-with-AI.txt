Role-Play Prompt — Defensive Employee
Use this prompt to simulate a challenging conversation with an employee who missed deadlines and responds with emotional pushback. The AI will hold its ground — and then coach you.
AI Prompt: "Act as a defensive employee who missed several deadlines. I am your manager. Push back respectfully but emotionally. Do not make the conversation easy. After 6 exchanges, switch to coaching observer and evaluate my performance."
After 6 exchanges, the AI evaluates you across five dimensions:
1
Clarity
Were your expectations clearly communicated?
2
Empathy
Did you acknowledge the employee's perspective?
3
Listening
Did you pause and respond to what was said?
4
Accountability
Did you hold the standard without attacking?
5
Follow-Up
Did you close with clear next steps?

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):
• This is a role-play exercise to practice difficult conversations with underperforming employees

• Use the AI prompt provided to set up a realistic scenario where the employee is defensive and emotional about missed deadlines

• The AI will push back respectfully — don't expect an easy conversation. That's the point.

• You'll have 6 exchanges with the AI before it switches roles

• After the role-play, the AI becomes a coaching observer and gives you feedback on five key areas:

Clarity: Did you communicate expectations clearly?

Empathy: Did you acknowledge their perspective and feelings?

Listening: Did you actually pause and respond to what they said, or just push your agenda?

Accountability: Did you maintain the standard without being harsh or personal?

Follow-Up: Did you end with concrete next steps and a clear path forward?

• This exercise helps you build skills for these tough conversations in a safe environment

Role-Play Prompt
Hesitant Employee
This scenario trains managers to create psychological safety — drawing out an employee who is afraid to speak honestly.
Key challenge: The employee only opens up if you ask the right questions with the right tone.
AI Prompt: "Act as a hesitant employee who is unsure whether it is safe to speak honestly. I am your manager. Be cautious at first. If I ask supportive open-ended questions, gradually share more. After 6 exchanges, give feedback on my tone, listening, and questions."
What the AI evaluates after the session:
Tone
Did your language feel safe and non-threatening?
Listening
Did you reflect back what the employee shared?
Questions
Were your questions open-ended and genuinely curious?

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):
• This is a role-play scenario designed to help you practice building psychological safety with your team

• You'll be interacting with an AI playing a hesitant employee — someone who's nervous about speaking up

• The key to success: ask the right questions in the right way. Your tone and approach will determine whether they open up

• Walk through the conversation naturally — you're trying to create an environment where it feels safe to be honest

• After 6 exchanges, the AI will give you feedback on three critical areas:

Tone: Did you sound approachable and non-threatening?

Listening: Did you actually reflect back what they said, or just move on?

Questions: Were you genuinely curious with open-ended questions, or did you ask leading/closed questions?

• Remember: the employee's willingness to share is your real-time feedback. If they're still guarded, adjust your approach

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):

• Monthly: Use this rhythm to inform your updates to development plans. You'll have real examples and observations fresh in your mind

Ethical Use Checklist
Before using AI in any coaching context, run through this checklist. Responsible use protects your employees, your organization, and your integrity as a leader.
Privacy Protected
I removed all private employee details before using AI
HR Boundaries Respected
I avoided entering sensitive HR or legal information
Bias Reviewed
I checked the AI output for potential bias or unfair framing
Accuracy Verified
I confirmed the content is factually accurate and appropriate
Humanized
I adapted the wording so it sounds like me, not a machine
Policy Compliant
I followed my organization's AI and data use policies
Final Decision Mine
I made the final judgment call — AI only informed it
Never paste identifiable employee information into any AI tool. Always anonymize first.

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):
• This checklist is your guardrail before deploying AI in any coaching situation. It protects your people, your organization, and your credibility.

• Privacy Protected — Strip out names, identifying details, anything that could trace back to a specific person before you paste anything into an AI tool.

• HR Boundaries Respected — Don't feed sensitive information into AI: performance ratings, disciplinary actions, salary data, legal issues. Keep those offline.

• Bias Reviewed — Read the AI output with a critical eye. Does it stereotype? Does it unfairly frame someone or a situation? Catch it before you use it.

• Accuracy Verified — Fact-check what the AI gives you. Make sure it's grounded in reality and appropriate for your context.

• Humanized — If it sounds robotic, rewrite it. Your coaching should sound like you, not like a machine generated it.

• Policy Compliant — Know your organization's rules on AI and data handling. Stay within bounds.

• Final Decision Mine — This is critical. AI is a tool that informs your judgment, not a replacement for it. You own the final call.

• Key reminder: Never, ever paste identifiable employee information into any AI tool. Anonymize first, always.
