Persona Scaffolding
Prompt scaffolding draws inspiration from educational scaffolding techniques, where support is provided to learners and gradually removed as they gain proficiency. Key aspects of AI Prompt scaffolding include:
Incremental Complexity: Gradually increasing the difficulty or depth of prompts.
Structured Guidance: Providing a clear framework for the AI to follow.
Step-by-Step Approach: Breaking down complex tasks into manageable steps.
Context Building: Progressively adding context and information.
Adaptive Support: Adjusting the level of guidance based on the AI's responses.

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):
• Think of prompt scaffolding like building construction—we're creating a framework of support that gets stronger as we go

• It's borrowed from education: teachers give students support, then gradually remove it as they become more capable. Same principle applies to prompting AI.

• Incremental Complexity: Don't ask everything at once. Start simple, then layer in more challenging requests as the AI demonstrates understanding.

• Structured Guidance: Give the AI a clear roadmap. Think of it like giving directions with landmarks rather than just saying "go that way."

• Step-by-Step Approach: Break big tasks into smaller chunks. Instead of "write a marketing campaign," try "first, identify the target audience, then develop three key messages, then create headlines."

• Context Building: Feed information gradually. Start with basic context, then add details, examples, or constraints as needed.

• Adaptive Support: Pay attention to how the AI responds. If it's nailing it, you can reduce guidance. If it's struggling, add more structure and examples.

• The key insight: better results come from building up systematically, not dumping everything into one massive prompt.

CINDEF Framework
A structured approach to crafting advanced prompts
Context
Set relevant background information
Instructions
Specify clear objectives and tasks
Details
Add specific parameters and constraints
Format
Define desired output structure

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):
• Introduce CINDEF as a practical framework for writing better AI prompts • Walk through each of the four components in order:

Context - Start by explaining the importance of background information. This helps the AI understand what situation or domain you're working in. It's like setting the stage for what comes next.

Instructions - These are your clear directives. Tell the AI exactly what you want it to do. Be specific about your objectives and the tasks you need completed.

Details - This is where you add the specifics. Include parameters, constraints, limitations, or any special requirements that will shape the output. Think of it as fine-tuning your request.

Format - End by specifying how you want the answer. Do you want a list? A paragraph? A table? Code? Being explicit about format saves time and gets you exactly what you need.

• Emphasize that this structured approach leads to more effective and reliable prompts • Note that these four elements work together—each one builds on the previous to create a complete, well-crafted prompt

From Basic to Prompt Engineering Level Quality:
Basic Prompt
Create a course on Management 101 concepts for middle managers.
Advanced Prompt (CINDEF Framework)
Persona: Adopt the role of a seasoned management trainer with 12 years of experience, specializing in developing leadership skills for middle managers.
Context: Use terms like 'decision-making frameworks' and 'team dynamics' and assume knowledge of basic business operations. Explain why this matters: As organizations evolve, middle managers play a pivotal role in bridging strategy and execution, making foundational management skills essential for organizational success.
Instructions: Break into 5 modules: 1. Core Management Principles 2. Effective Decision-Making 3. Enhancing Team Dynamics 4. Communication and Influence 5. Managing Change and Innovation.
Details: Use action verbs: 'Implement,' 'Facilitate,' 'Drive.' Include success metrics: 'Participants must demonstrate improved team engagement scores by 20%.' Mandatory: Use real-world scenarios and management case studies. Tone: Practical and empowering. Avoid overly academic language. Required sections: Learning objectives, interactive activities, self-assessment tools.
Format: Course outline with modules, learning objectives, and delivery suggestions.

Speaker notes (how to teach this slide):
• Show the contrast: Basic prompt gets basic output—generic, surface-level content. Advanced prompt gets structured, tailored, high-quality results.

• Left side—Basic approach: Just asking for a course without specifics. Results in generic modules, no clear learning outcomes, lacks practical application.

• Right side—Advanced approach using CINDEF Framework:

Persona: Give the AI a role—experienced trainer with 12 years in leadership development. This shapes the tone and depth.

Context: Provide background on why this matters—middle managers bridge strategy and execution. Use industry language they'll understand.

Instructions: Be specific about structure—5 distinct modules with clear progression.

Details: Define what success looks like—20% improvement in engagement scores. Specify tone (practical, not academic), required elements (case studies, self-assessments).

Format: Clarify exactly what you want to receive.

• Key takeaway: The more specific and structured your prompt, the better the output. CINDEF gives you a repeatable framework to engineer prompts that deliver professional-quality results every time.
